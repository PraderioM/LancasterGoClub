The most commonly played variant of Go is usually played in an initially empty 19x19 board and the goal is that of getting more territory than your enemy. The concept of territory is however a bit difficult to understand if you have never played before and a 19x19 board can feel intimidating at first. That is why, for your first games, we recommend you to play an easier and faster to play variant named Capture Go. Here are the 5 things you need to know in order to play.

1) Setup

Each player chooses a color (black or white) and takes all stones of that color. Then you start the game on an empty 9x9 board with the configuration shown below.

2) Placing stones

Black plays first. Black and white take turns placing one stone at a time on an empty intersection (not in the squares!). Once a stone is played it cannot be moved. Stones can also be placed in the intersections of corners and edges.

3) Liberties of groups

Stones of the same color that touch each other along a line form a group. The intersections connected to a group along a line are called it's liberties.

4) Capturing

As soon as a group of stones has no liberties that group is captured and removed off the board. Note that a stone can be placed where it has no liberties as long as, by doing so, it captures the opponent.

5) How to win

The winner is the first player to capture one or more stones.
